Technologies: Cyber Security

Cybersecurity is one of, if not, the fastest growing sector within the Information Technology (IT) industry.

So, what is cybersecurity? Cyber security, by definition, is the application of technologies, processes, and controls to protect systems, devices, and data from cyber-attacks. These cyberattacks are normally aimed at extorting money from users, disrupting normal operations of businesses and accessing or deleting sensitive information. Successful cybersecurity applications are usually multi-layered, spread across computers, networks, data, or programs. Both the people as well as all processes and technologies within an organisation must compliment each other to build an effective threat management system. The people within any organisation must understand and be aware of basic security principles such as having a secure password, being mindful of unusual emails containing attachments and conscious of backing up data. Having good processes in place is crucial to managing cyberattacks. This should be in the format of a framework for employees to follow when dealing with Successful or attempted cyberattacks. There are ready-made frameworks, such as the NIST cybersecurity framework by CISCO, that organisations can use to help employees identify attacks, protect, detect, and respond to cyberattacks. Advancements in technologies within the cybersecurity sector are crucial in what is now a technology-driven world. In the last 10 years alone, cybercrimes have evolved from a niche threat carried out by specialists to what is now the biggest threat the world faces. In the next few years there are 3 modern technologies that may define the future of cybersecurity. These technologies include 5G networks and infrastructure, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Biometrics.

The introduction of 5G networks and infrastructure across the globe has become the single greatest challenge for the cybersecurity sector and opens a world of opportunity for cyber criminals. Along with internet 1000x faster, the design and rollout of the 5G networks across the world means there will be over 75 billion new devices, all running important infrastructure and apps, connected to the internet every year from 2025 onwards. This could drastically change the current threat landscape, meaning cybercrime could move from being relatively unknown and financially driven to more real damage being done at a much faster pace. The new 5G networks could give cyber criminals almost instant access to any vulnerable network in the world. If these new networks are then combined with enterprise technology, we could see a new type of cyberattacks occurring. A recent example of this is a cyberattack that occurred in Baltimore, USA; this resulted in over 10,000 employees being locked out of their workstations. This is concerning as; the near future appears to be turning to the concept of “smart cities.” These “smart cities” create a system of networks such as transportation for self-driving cars, automated water and waste systems, as well as emergency services and workers. All these systems are as interdependent and as potentially vulnerable to cybercrimes as they are highly connected. The new 5G networks will obviously open new chances and avenues for prosperity and growth around the world, however it also opens the world up to new threats and levels of cybercrimes that we have not seen before, nor do we know the potential ramifications of these threats.

One of the newest and greatest defences the globe has against cyberattacks is AI. The biggest positive about AI is being able to safeguard and protect the world’s digital freedom against an evolving threat at an exceptional pace and scale. As there is such a significant need for efficiencies in areas such as detection, situational awareness, and ability to react to threats in real-time as they occur, AI and AI-driven programs will become the future of defense against cybersecurity threats on any scale. However, AI comes with its own set of risks. Currently, most new developments in AI for cybersecurity have been quickly obtained by

the cyber-criminal community and repurposed to be a threat rather than a defense. Meaning AI is both the greatest development in cybersecurity defense technology as well as one of the greatest threats to the digital world. Criminals are exploiting AI to bypass layers and years of advancements in cyber defences; in an instant. It is believed that the financial sector will begin to see criminals deploy malware that makes use of voice capture and synthesizing technology and biometric data to work around authentication controls in place to protect bank accounts. Although this is just one example, criminal use of AI will be targeted and deployed for optimal effect and present a new threat no-one was prepared for being the theft and storing of biological material such as DNA. It is likely we will see the hacking and hijacking of autonomous vehicles and ransomware attacks on healthcare and emergency services systems more frequently.

The third and final technology is biometrics. Biometrics has been widely introduced around the globe within different sectors and with different goals whilst also introducing new challenges to the world's cybersecurity community. Biometrics along with other new authentication technologies require a huge amount of someone's personal data such as their behaviour, activity, and DNA. All this data must be stored worldwide thus presenting itself as a target for well organised cyberattacks. This will lead to a whole new level of theft, not just targeting someone’s credit card numbers but instead their personal data and their identity. Still though, security experts believe that three-step authentication is the most secure way forward. This means you need to know your password, have a code and be able to provide some form of biometric identification. The cybersecurity community must, however, continue to evolve and find new means and technologies to defend the globe’s digital information.

With all these recent changes in security technologies, it will become incredibly difficult for myself, my family, my friends, and everyone globally to be secure online. Knowing that 5G networks will make it significantly faster for cyberattacks to spread is worrying, however it is the role of all users to keep their accounts, data and personal information secure by changing passwords consistently and avoiding suspicious emails and text messages.

Technologies: Cloud, Services, Server

Server is a computer program or device that accepts and responds to requests made by another program, it can be setup based on your different needs and functions, such as, to control access to a network, send/receive e-mail, manage print jobs, or host a website. Every time you use the internet you are accessing a server. When you enter a URL into a browser your computer communicates with the server hosting that website and pulls the data onto your machine. People can connect to a server through either a local area network or a wide area network, such as the Internet.

Cloud computing needs servers to function, it is a general term for anything that involves the delivery of computing resources and a series of IT services over internet, including Email, storage, backup, and data retrieval, creating and testing apps, analysing data, audio and video streaming, online software.

It is named as such because the information and functions being accessed is found remotely in the servers or a virtual space. Companies that provide Cloud services enable users to store files and applications on remote servers and then access all the data via the Internet. This means the user is not required to be in a specific place to gain access to it, allowing the user to work remotely.

As Cloud has become more popular, it offers different services based on three models: SaaS, PaaS and IaaS. Service as a service (SaaS) is a service offers on-demand use of application to users, it is an independent platform that doesn’t need to install software on computer and it’s available for multiple users. This type of service is accessible via a web browser or a lightweight application. PaaS is mainly a development environment and is made up of a program language execution environment, an operating system, as web server and database, all of these encapsulate the environment where users can build, compile and run their program. IaaS offers the computing architecture and infrastructure in a virtual environment so that multiple users can access them.

The advantage of the Cloud is that you can access your information on any device with an internet connection. For instance, it allows you to make edits to a file in Google Docs on your home computer, and then pick up where you left off when you get to the office. Colleagues can even collaborate on the same document, because the documents are hosted in the cloud, not on individual computers, an Internet connection is all that is needed to collaborate.

It also gives you easy access to a broad range of technologies so that you can innovate faster and build nearly anything that you can imagine. You can quickly spin up resources as you need them from infrastructure services.

With the Cloud, you can expand to new geographic regions, for example, AWS has infrastructure all over the world, so you can install your application in multiple physical locations. Organizations of any type, size, and industry are using the Cloud for a wide variety of use cases, healthcare companies are using the Cloud to develop more personalized treatments for patients; financial services companies are using the Cloud to power real-time fraud detection and prevention. And video game makers are using the Cloud to deliver online games to millions of players around the world.

But the Cloud has its downfalls as well. Without an Internet connection you’re basically locked out of accessing your data and cloud-based programs. The same applies if there are any technical issues or outages on the server side. Also, because your information lives online, there’s always the risk of it getting into the wrong hands. All cloud companies have security measures in place to protect your data from hackers.

For me, the application of Cloud services has significantly impacted on my study, work and my daily life. One close example for me at work is through the use of Outlook and Gmail services, instead of organizing my mails on my laptop locally, these mail services with various features enable me to do it online by just having internet connection. Another example of such Cloud service is GitHub, this is a platform for all programmers to be able to manage and collaborate their codes and projects online.

Before the concept of Cloud, we were stuck only being able to watch what was shown on the television with our friends and family. But since Cloud has been invented, it revolutionized how entertainment is delivered to us, there are many stream services like Netflix and Apple music that give us more option on what we want to watch and listen whenever we have internet connection. All these contents are delivered using Cloud technology.

Blockchain Technology 

What does it do? 

 

Blockchain is a list of records that are linked together using encryption. The chain consists of many blocks that contain transaction data and a timestamp. As many blocks are created they link together and form a chain, no block can be removed from this chain, and each block that precedes the one before it strengthens the chain. 

 

The technology runs on peer-to-peer networks where the information is stored on individual computers and linked together using the internet. It aims to replace or provide an alternate means of currency to the current system of currency validated by various governments. 

What is the state of the art of this new technology?  

 

Blockchain delivers information securely and transparently via immutable ledgers that can be accessed by members that are part of an agreed network. Blockchain can keep track of transactions from start to finish with reliability. Tracking payment information, account information, and any other financial information. 

 

The technology has been around since 2008 created by an individual or a group of people under the name “Satoshi Nakamoto”. The technology is currently being used by a wide variety of industries some of which include; digital art, agriculture, finance, security, and gaming. 

 

What can be done now? 

 

 The technology also implements smart contracts which allow users to create digital contracts based on the blockchain. Which enforces decentralized agreements that are encrypted and often automated (for automatic transaction agreements). Tampering a transaction or transfer after it has been logged in the blockchain creates an error in the chain. If this is a case it must be rectified via a new transaction. Which is then displayed on the blockchain along with the previous one. 

 

Many items are being “tokenized” and made unique via the use of NFT’s (Non- Fungible Tokens). These “mint” digital art declaring them unique and place them on the blockchain, which users can then bid on and purchase creating a value determined by social value. Because there is only one digital item, akin to the Mona Lisa, the item is deemed to have high value and is sought to be purchased by many online art investors. 

 

Immutable records make sure the parties involved have secure access to the record of transactions. Everything is recorded accurately once, eliminating any error via duplicate records or misinformation. 

 

What is likely to be able to do be done soon? 

 

Digital Identity Technology can be created to easily manage and secure user identity. This will be created via the assignment of a random set of numbers to a user on a blockchain network. Making it near impossible to hack without access to the private user key. Much safer than the current username and password solution to user account security. 

 

Blockchain will help the distribution and verification of drugs and other health equipment. Each item can be coded onto the blockchain ledger to create a unique item identity, making sure there are no counterfeits in the supply chain. This ensures safer drug delivery as it can be verified with the blockchain instead of a range of tracking details as it operates currently(Eg. The Covid-19 Vaccines). 

 

In the automotive and housing industry, you would be able to purchase assets via cryptocurrency and smart contracts in a matter of minutes. Instead of the long process of verifying an individual's credit and financial suitability to purchase assets, the user can be verified using the blockchain sharing their credentials with the seller prior to purchase. This will help get rid of the conventional credit score system and will lead to fairer mortgage offers and loan policies. 

 

What is the likely impact? 

 

Blockchain technology is growing at a rate of 45%, it is expected to reach USD 34Billion by 2026. The scope of blockchain can expand to any industry this includes law enforcement, the legal system, finance, and item consumption. 

 

Digital identities, consumer purchasing, and selling systems, management, logistics, and the transactions of the Internet of Things are current and future applications of blockchain all will be affected greatly. In ways, we may not even be able to currently comprehend. 

 

Blockchain is already dismantling financial systems by providing people with an alternative way to purchase and invest in stocks and options. It will continue to do so, forcing the banks to create and innovate their own blockchain technologies. Giving the consumers an altogether more secure system for financial transactions with newer technology. 

Digital art has been impacted by many popular musical artists creating their own NFT tokens and selling them for thousands of dollars. Along with physical items containing NFT codes that are an exclusive purchase from an album or project that they recently have released. Artists such as; The Weeknd, Soulja Boy, Grimes, and Steve Aoki have all successfully launched and sold NFTs as part of album releases. Including Digital Lifetime Tickets, that let you access any show of the artist in the future, physical merchandise, and exclusive album art.  

How will this affect you? 

 

The changes that Blockchain will make to the music industry will be highly significant. The entire premise of musicians getting signed to record labels to enter the market and "verify" their music will be an outdated notion. This is because music artists will now be able to source capital directly from their fans using NFT's and smart contracts. Allowing their fans to invest in their music directly. 

 

My father's business will also be affected directly as he is an innovator in Agricultural technology. He provides verification grading of food products using AI and quality detection. His certificates of food grading will now be blockchain-based and be completed via smart contracts. 

 

Family and friends will be impacted with a variety of financial options for investing long term. My mother and sister have already enquired how to purchase the Cryptocurrency Ethereum to invest long term. 

 

Schools and Universities may switch their grading systems to be based on Blockchain technologies. This would allow records to be secure and even safer than standard encryption. 

 

Even something as simple as paying for groceries may change due to Blockchain technology. As credit cards may be released or payment methods may be introduced that are cryptocurrency-friendly and convert the Crypto token value into standard currency.

Arduino and Raspberry Pi 

What does it do? 

 

Arduino is an easy-to-use electronics board and platform that comes with software to program instructions. The board has many input options and it can turn on various outputs either physically eg. a motor, or digitally eg. publishing a document online. The Arduino is programmed via wiring and the software is programmed via an IDE. 

 

Arduino has been used for many purposes, from general objects to advanced robotics. Students and professionals from many fields use and contribute to Arduino's open-source platform. This helps the platform continue to develop and helps all levels of programmers. 

 

What is the state of the art of this new technology?  

 

Arduino was initially aimed at students learning programming and electronics. Created by the Ivrea Interaction Design Institute. However, it reached a wider range of users with applications in 3D printing, the Internet of Things (IoT), and many more environments. Due to this, it was adapted from an 8-bit board into a device that could handle much more complex capabilities. 

 

Raspberry Pi is a credit card-sized computer that can plug into a monitor or TV, it uses a standalone keyboard and mouse for access. It's a fairly powerful device for its size and can handle multiple programming languages like Scratch and Python. It can also do regular things that a normal PC is able to, such as video games, video viewing, and word processing. 

 

What can be done now? 

 

With many possible applications, Raspberry Pi has been used in all sorts of interesting and quirky projects. From birdhouses to weather stations, personal desk assistants, and more. Raspberry Pi is being tested and used by people looking to innovate and get started with computing applications in real life. 

 

The Raspberry Pi is an educational charity, that aims to advance the education of children and adults in the field of computer science. 

 

Arduino and Raspberry Pi together contain boards and modules, as well as shields that extend the functionality of the boards. The Atmel family microcontroller in Arduino extends the capabilities of the board allowing even more CPU and programmable options. Both Raspberry Pi and Arduino contain input and output allowing you to attach sensors to them. Both have software components and IDE’s to program the functionality of the devices. 

 

 

Arduino utilizes C++ and Raspberry Pi uses Python mainly. Both IDEs can be programmed to work together. Utilizing the computing power of the Raspberry Pi for intelligence-related tasks and the functionality of the Arduino for robotics and physical purposes. 

 

What is likely to be able to do be done soon? 

 

The Internet of Things (IoT) is going to make everyday devices insanely capable of things we have only witnessed in movies. Clothing, mirrors, and perhaps even consumables may become intelligently interactive with the user. Via input and processing, some of these devices may even have a neural network that enables them to learn what the user prefers depending on the circumstance. 

 

What is the likely impact? What is the likely impact?  

 

Arduino kits are developed solely for participating in the IoT revolution. Raspberry Pi’s are geared also towards IoT however in a more computer intelligence-related focus.  They both come with Wifi modules that let you build applications that can be connected to the cloud or other devices.  

 

Unlike the Raspberry Pi, which mainly offers a credit-card-sized solution for computing, Arduino provides both personal and industrial solutions in their products. With a variety of specifications for different purposes. Shields add functionality to the motherboards and are extensions that can be added to existing Arduino’s, this helps them utilize things such as ethernet. 

 

Both of these products are quite affordable and create a platform for innovators to experiment and try out new ideas effectively and for a lower cost, compared to other standard industrial solutions.
